Thursday TBL: investigate the differential

Five options. Anatomy decides.

Each option below could produce neonatal failure to pass meconium with a distal obstruction pattern. Your team's job is to anatomically distinguish them using GI wall layers, embryologic origin, and the biopsy findings.

Option Anatomic site / mechanism Distinguishing feature
Hirschsprung disease Absent enteric ganglia (Meissner + Auerbach) Squirt sign + transition zone + biopsy showing aganglionosis
Meconium ileus Distal small bowel obstruction by inspissated meconium Almost always cystic fibrosis, ileal-level obstruction, no transition zone
Anal atresia Failed cloacal membrane recanalization Absent or abnormal anal opening on inspection, mechanical obstruction
Functional constipation Normal anatomy, behavioral Older infants and children, normal biopsy, responds to laxatives
Volvulus / malrotation Failed embryologic intestinal rotation Bilious emesis but with corkscrew on contrast study, surgical emergency

Team task

  1. For each option, locate the anatomic site on your master diagram.
  2. Mark which case findings are consistent and inconsistent with each option.
  3. Pick number one. Defend it using the anatomic discriminator.
  4. Be ready to explain why each rejected option does not fit, anatomically.
