Thursday TBL: investigate the differential

Five options. Anatomy decides.

Each option could produce a tall body habitus with connective tissue findings. Your team's job is to anatomically distinguish them using the distribution of the affected connective tissue protein and the constellation of organ findings.

Option Anatomic site / mechanism Distinguishing feature
Marfan syndrome Fibrillin-1 (FBN1) defect, microfibrils Skeletal + ocular (lens) + cardiovascular (aorta), AD inheritance
Homocystinuria Cystathionine beta-synthase deficiency Lens dislocates downward (vs Marfan upward), thrombosis risk, intellectual disability
Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome Other collagen subtypes (III, V) Skin hyperextensibility, easy bruising, less skeletal overgrowth
Loeys-Dietz Syndrome TGF-beta receptor mutations Bifid uvula, hypertelorism, more aggressive aortic disease
Constitutional tall stature (no syndrome) Normal connective tissue No extra-skeletal connective tissue findings, no family history of sudden death

Team task

  1. For each option, locate the anatomic site on your master diagram.
  2. Mark which case findings are consistent and inconsistent with each option.
  3. Pick number one. Defend it using the anatomic discriminator.
  4. Be ready to explain why each rejected option does not fit, anatomically.
