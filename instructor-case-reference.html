01Unknown Case 1
Week 1 · June 15 to 18
Actual diagnosis
Burn injury
Module 1 · Introduction, terminology, cell, tissues, integumentary

Structural teaching points

  • Skin layers: epidermis, dermis, hypodermis
  • Burn depth classification (superficial, partial thickness, full thickness) mapped to tissue layers
  • Rule of Nines for body surface area using anatomical regions and terminology
  • Parkland formula for fluid resuscitation
  • Cellular response to thermal injury

Expected differentials for students to explore

  • Contact dermatitis or allergic reaction
  • Cellulitis or necrotizing fasciitis
  • Scald injury vs flame burn vs chemical burn
  • Non accidental injury consideration in pediatric or dependent adult cases
02Unknown Case 2
Week 2 · June 22 to 25
Actual diagnosis
Osteogenesis imperfecta with fractures
Module 2 Part 1 · Cartilage, bone tissue, axial skeleton

Structural teaching points

  • Type I collagen defect (COL1A1 or COL1A2)
  • Bone tissue microanatomy: osteon, lamellae, osteocytes
  • Fracture patterns and healing differences in abnormal bone matrix
  • Axial skeleton involvement (blue sclera, dentinogenesis imperfecta, skull fragility)

Expected differentials

  • Non accidental trauma (must be seriously considered in pediatric fractures)
  • Nutritional rickets or vitamin D deficiency
  • Juvenile idiopathic osteoporosis
  • Other genetic bone dysplasias
03Unknown Case 3
Week 3 · June 29 to July 2
Actual diagnosis
Marfan syndrome
Module 2 Part 2 · Axial column, appendicular, joints

Structural teaching points

  • Fibrillin-1 gene defect (FBN1)
  • Connective tissue structural weakness throughout the skeleton
  • Axial: scoliosis, pectus excavatum or carinatum
  • Appendicular: long limbs, arachnodactyly, positive wrist and thumb signs
  • Joints: hypermobility, recurrent dislocations

Expected differentials

  • Homocystinuria (similar skeletal findings, different genetics)
  • Ehlers-Danlos syndrome
  • MASS phenotype
  • Familial tall stature without pathology
04Unknown Case 4
Week 4 · July 6 to 9
Actual diagnosis
Tetralogy of Fallot
Module 3 Part 1 · Heart, respiratory

Structural teaching points

  • Single embryological conotruncal septation defect
  • Four structural abnormalities: ventricular septal defect, overriding aorta, pulmonary stenosis, right ventricular hypertrophy
  • Right to left shunt producing cyanosis
  • Respiratory consequences and hypoxic spells

Expected differentials

  • Other cyanotic congenital heart disease: transposition of the great arteries, truncus arteriosus, total anomalous pulmonary venous return, tricuspid atresia
  • Respiratory causes of cyanosis in an infant
  • Methemoglobinemia
05Unknown Case 5
Week 5 · July 13 to 16
Actual diagnosis
Sickle cell disease with splenic and lymphoid complications
Module 3 Part 2 · Lymphatic, blood

Structural teaching points

  • Hemoglobin beta chain gene defect producing HbS
  • Sickled red blood cell structural abnormality
  • Vaso occlusion and microinfarction across multiple organs
  • Splenic sequestration, infarction, and autosplenectomy over time
  • Reactive lymphadenopathy from increased infection susceptibility after functional asplenia

Expected differentials

  • Other hemoglobinopathies: thalassemia, HbC disease
  • Autoimmune hemolytic anemia
  • Lymphoma (lymphadenopathy can mislead)
  • Hereditary spherocytosis
  • Acute chest syndrome vs pneumonia in an exacerbation
06Unknown Case 6
Week 6 · July 20 to 23
Actual diagnosis
Hirschsprung disease
Module 4 Part 1 · Endocrine, gastrointestinal

Structural teaching points

  • Failure of neural crest cell migration during embryological development
  • Absent enteric ganglion cells (Meissner and Auerbach plexuses) in the distal colon
  • Structural bowel wall differences between affected and proximal dilated segments
  • Transition zone on imaging
  • Connection between GI wall anatomy and enteric nervous system development

Expected differentials

  • Meconium ileus (cystic fibrosis association)
  • Anorectal malformations
  • Functional constipation
  • Intestinal atresia or stenosis
  • Neonatal sepsis with ileus
07Unknown Case 7
Week 7 · July 27 to 30
Actual diagnosis
Polycystic kidney disease (ADPKD)
Module 4 Part 2 · Renal, reproductive

Structural teaching points

  • PKD1 or PKD2 gene mutations
  • Multiple renal cortical and medullary cysts, progressive kidney enlargement
  • Extra renal manifestations: liver cysts, seminal vesicle cysts in males, epididymal cysts
  • Intracranial aneurysm association
  • Structural changes that can present with pelvic pain and palpable masses

Expected differentials to rule out

  • Simple renal cysts (benign, single or few)
  • Ovarian cysts (can dominate the initial presentation in a female patient with pelvic pain)
  • Uterine fibroids
  • Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS, the reproductive only version)
  • Primary seminal vesicle cyst unrelated to PKD
  • Renal cell carcinoma
  • Acquired cystic kidney disease (in patients on dialysis)
Teaching note on reproductive distractors Female patients with PKD may initially present with pelvic pain or palpable abdominal mass that gets worked up as gynecologic pathology (ovarian cyst, fibroid) before imaging reveals the renal cysts. Male patients may present with infertility or scrotal discomfort from seminal vesicle or epididymal involvement. This demonstrates how a primarily renal disease can masquerade as reproductive pathology and reinforces the shared intermediate mesoderm embryological origin of the two systems.
08Unknown Case 8
Week 8 · August 3 to 6
Actual diagnosis
Normal pressure hydrocephalus
Module 5 · Nervous system

Structural teaching points

  • CSF circulation pathway: lateral ventricles, third ventricle, cerebral aqueduct, fourth ventricle, subarachnoid space
  • Impaired CSF absorption through arachnoid granulations
  • Progressive ventricular dilation despite normal opening pressure
  • Periventricular white matter tracts compressed by ventricular expansion
  • Meninges and CSF spaces

Clinical triad

  • Gait disturbance (magnetic gait)
  • Cognitive impairment
  • Urinary incontinence

Expected differentials

  • Obstructive (non communicating) hydrocephalus
  • Cerebral atrophy (Alzheimer disease, vascular dementia)
  • Parkinson disease (gait similar)
  • Brain tumor with mass effect
  • Chronic subdural hematoma
  • Cervical spinal stenosis (gait)
Reversibility note NPH is one of the few reversible causes of dementia. Ventriculoperitoneal shunt placement can improve symptoms. Worth noting for student presentations and for the broader clinical reasoning lesson that not every dementia presentation is irreversible.
