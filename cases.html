BIO 004 Weekly Cases

Case Framework

Eight unknown cases. Four days of structured anatomical reasoning per week. Built on the principle that clinical data is given, and the thinking is what students bring.

How Each Week Runs

Every week follows the same four-day arc. Students receive clinical data progressively. Their job is not to research what each finding means from scratch but to annotate each piece of information with the anatomical and light-physiological reasoning that explains it.

Given, not Googled. Labs, imaging, physical exam findings, and vital signs arrive pre-populated with values and normal ranges. Students annotate in the margins: what anatomy is involved, what function is disrupted, and how the two connect. Study time goes toward anatomical reasoning, not encyclopedic research.
1Mon
Case Intro
Meet the patient. Team forms an initial differential.
  • Demographics and chief complaint
  • History of present illness
  • Vital signs and initial physical exam
  • Week's anatomical focus identified
  • Team generates 3 to 5 initial differentials
2Tue
Case Prep
Labs released. Team annotates and refines.
  • Additional history: family, social, medications
  • Lab results with normal ranges, pre-populated
  • Students annotate each lab with anatomy it points to
  • Differential narrows or expands based on data
  • Gaps in reasoning identified for Wednesday
3Wed
Case Run
Imaging released. Team works the application questions.
  • Radiology and imaging findings released
  • Students annotate each finding with structures involved
  • Application questions worked through in teams
  • Differential narrowed to top 2 or 3
  • Presentation reasoning prepared for Thursday
4Thu
TBL and Case Presentation
IRAT, TRAT, presentations, class discussion, reveal.
  • IRAT, 10 to 15 questions, individual, weighted 3x
  • TRAT, same quiz, team, weighted 1x
  • Teams present their differentials and reasoning
  • Class discussion facilitated by Dr. Rennie
  • Actual diagnosis revealed, anatomy debriefed

The Case Note Sheet

Each case comes with a handwritten case note sheet students fill in during the week. The sheet contains pre-populated clinical data with space to annotate. Structure of each sheet:

Patient Information
Demographics, chief complaint, HPI, pertinent history. Pre-populated on release.
Clinical Data
Vital signs, physical exam findings, labs with normal ranges, imaging results. Given values; students annotate.
Anatomy Annotation Column
For each finding: which structure is involved, what function is disrupted, how anatomy explains the presentation.
Differential and Reasoning
Space for 3 to 5 differentials with justification tied to anatomy. Top 2 to 3 by Wednesday. Final reasoning by Thursday.
