1Grade Structure Summary

The Clinical Professionalism grade is 10% of the course grade. Two inputs combine:

Input Weight within CP grade Percent of course grade
Instructor evaluation (you) Two thirds 6.67%
Peer evaluation (teammates) One third 3.33%
Total 100% 10%

Evaluation cycles split evenly within the CP grade:

  • Week 4 midterm cycle: 50% of CP grade (5 points of 10)
  • Week 8 final cycle: 50% of CP grade (5 points of 10)

Each cycle evaluates the preceding four weeks of observation.

2Peer Review: Fillable PDF Workflow

Students use a browser-based fillable form to evaluate each teammate. One form per teammate. Students save the completed form as a PDF and upload to Canvas.

File used by students

clinical-professionalism-peer-review.html

Host this file where students can access it: Canvas page embed, GitHub Pages URL, or direct file download. Students open in any modern browser.

Student workflow

For each evaluation cycle (Midterm and Final):

  1. Student opens the peer review form in a browser
  2. Fills in own name, team, selects Midterm or Final cycle, signs, dates
  3. Types first teammate's name in the ocean-deep banner at the top
  4. Clicks a rating (4 / 3 / 2 / 1) for each of four dimensions
  5. Writes justification if required (all 4s, or any 1)
  6. Clicks Save as PDF button
  7. In browser's print dialog, selects "Save as PDF" as destination
  8. Saves PDF with auto-suggested filename PeerEval_[RaterLast]_rates_[TeammateLast]_[Cycle].pdf
  9. Uploads PDF to Canvas peer evaluation assignment
  10. Clicks Start next teammate button (keeps own info, clears teammate fields)
  11. Repeats steps 3 through 9 for next teammate
  12. Continues until all teammates evaluated. Submits N total PDFs where N equals number of teammates

What the form captures per teammate

  • Rater's name, team, cycle, date, signature
  • Teammate's name being evaluated
  • Four dimension rubric ratings (Preparation, Participation, Professionalism, Growth), scored 1 to 4 each
  • Auto-calculated raw score (out of 16) and percentage
  • Justification text (required if extreme ratings triggered)
  • Date submitted

Canvas setup

Create two Canvas assignments (one per cycle):

Assignment: "CP Peer Review, Midterm"
  • Due date: Saturday end of day following Week 4 Thursday TBL
  • Points: 0 (non-gradebook. You post the computed CP grade separately)
  • Submission type: File upload
  • Allowed file types: PDF
  • Multiple file uploads: Enabled (students submit one PDF per teammate)
  • Instructions: Link to peer review form, explain one-PDF-per-teammate rule
Assignment: "CP Peer Review, Final"
  • Same setup as Midterm
  • Due date: Saturday end of day following Week 8 Thursday TBL

What to do with submitted PDFs

  1. Download all PDFs from Canvas (use bulk download option)
  2. Filename convention makes sorting easy: PeerEval_[rater]_rates_[teammate]_[cycle].pdf
  3. Group by teammate (who is being evaluated) rather than by rater
  4. Read each teammate's incoming evaluations. Enter rating data into spreadsheet (see Section 3)
  5. Moderate for fairness (see Section 3, Step 3)
  6. Archive the PDFs in a secured folder for chain of custody

Instructor's own rubric follows the same workflow

You use clinical-professionalism-rubric-instructor.html, which follows the same form pattern:

  1. Open form in browser
  2. Fill student name, team, select cycle
  3. Click ratings for each dimension
  4. Watch auto-calculated totals update (Raw score, %, Cycle Points out of 5)
  5. Fill evidence counts (HW bundles, app exercises, attendance, violations)
  6. Add observations and justification
  7. Sign
  8. Click Save as PDF. Filename saves as CP_Rubric_[StudentLast]_[Cycle].pdf
  9. Optionally upload to Canvas as instructor comment on the student's grade entry
  10. Click Clear form for the next student

Do this twice per student (midterm and final). Total instructor PDFs per semester: student count multiplied by 2. For 30 students, that is 60 PDFs.

3Calculation Workflow

Per-student calculation (run once per cycle, per student).

Step 1: Instructor raw score

From your Clinical Professionalism Instructor Rubric, total the four dimension ratings (1 to 4 each) to get raw score out of 16. Convert to percentage:

Instructor % = (Raw score / 16) × 100

Step 2: Peer average score

Collect all peer ratings for this student from submitted PDFs. Average across all evaluators. If the student has four teammates, four evaluations contribute. Each evaluation has four ratings (Preparation, Participation, Professionalism, Growth), each out of 4.

Per evaluator raw = sum of 4 dimensions, out of 16
Per evaluator %   = (raw / 16) × 100
Peer average %    = average of all evaluator %s

Step 3: Moderate outliers

Before averaging peer scores, spot check for these patterns:

  • One extreme rating in a pattern of moderate ratings. If three teammates rate a student "Usually" or "Sometimes" and one rates "Rarely," scrutinize the outlier. Read the justification. If the justification is weak or suggests retaliation or bias, discount the outlier from the peer average.
  • All 4s from one teammate across all peers rated. Probably a student who rated everyone generously. Not punitive, but flag for caution in interpretation.
  • Clearly retaliatory patterns. If Student A rated Student B poorly AND Student B rated Student A poorly, check the justifications on both. Discount either, both, or neither based on evidence.

Document any moderation decisions briefly for your records.

Step 4: Combine into CP cycle score

CP cycle % = (Instructor % × 2/3) + (Peer average % × 1/3)

Step 5: Convert to cycle points (out of 5)

Cycle points = (CP cycle % / 100) × 5

Step 6: Calculate final CP grade at end of course

Final CP grade (out of 10) = Midterm cycle points + Final cycle points

Worked example

Example calculation

Midterm instructor ratings: Preparation 4, Participation 3, Professionalism 4, Growth 3. Raw = 14/16 = 87.5%.

Four peer evaluators gave percentages of 88%, 75%, 90%, 82%. Peer average = 83.75%.

Midterm CP % = (87.5 × 2/3) + (83.75 × 1/3)
             = 58.33 + 27.92
             = 86.25%

Midterm cycle points = (86.25 / 100) × 5 = 4.31 / 5

If this student earns similar scores at final:

Final CP grade = 4.31 + 4.31 = 8.62 / 10 (86.2%)

That translates to 8.62% of the 100% course grade.

5Weekly Flag Check Protocol

Not graded. Runs every Thursday after TBL. Purpose: early warning for student or team issues before they compound.

Simple Google Form (or paper slip)

For each team member except self: "Pulled their weight this week? Green / Yellow / Red."

Interpretation

  • All green across the team: Team is healthy. No action.
  • One or two yellows scattered: Normal variation. No action unless repeated.
  • Two yellows on the same student in consecutive weeks: Send a brief private email to check in.
  • Three consecutive yellows OR any red: Schedule a private conversation. Document the date.
  • Full-team red from one student: Separate issue. Address individually. May indicate reshuffling need.
Confidentiality Flag check data is NOT shared with the rated student. It is your early warning system.

6Policy Quick Reference

What students see in the syllabus

  • 10% of grade
  • Split two thirds instructor plus one third peer
  • Rated on four dimensions (Preparation, Participation, Professionalism, Growth)
  • Two evaluation cycles (Week 4 midterm, Week 8 final)
  • Rubric is published. They see the same one you use.
  • Peer evaluations are confidential to Dr. Rennie
  • Weekly flag check is not graded

What students do NOT see

  • Your moderation decisions
  • Specific peer ratings of them
  • The observation tracker

Student responsibilities

  • Complete homework bundles on schedule
  • Participate in Wednesday application exercises
  • Show up prepared, on time, professional
  • Complete peer review within the 48 hour window at Week 4 and Week 8
  • Justify extreme ratings with specific observations

Appeal process

If a student disputes their CP grade:

  1. Student requests a meeting in writing within 7 days of grade posting
  2. Meeting is held to review the rubric ratings and general reasoning without exposing specific peer raters
  3. Instructor's ratings, peer average percentage, and moderation notes are the basis for discussion
  4. If a clerical error is found, grade is corrected. If not, grade stands.
  5. Peer identities remain confidential throughout the process.

8Common Scenarios

A student misses one peer review window (didn't submit) They receive a zero on the 1/3 peer contribution they were supposed to submit. Their own CP grade still receives the peer input from teammates who DID rate them. Document the missed submission.
A student is absent during flag check Thursday No action needed. Flag check is not graded. If absence patterns emerge, note in observation tracker.
A student rates everyone identically (all 4s, or all 2s) at peer review Not automatically invalidated, but treated with caution. Look at whether the ratings make sense with observed team behavior. If the rater is trying to avoid giving honest feedback, moderate accordingly.
A teammate writes an angry or retaliatory justification Document it. Consider discounting that rating from the peer average for that teammate. If the justification suggests a serious interpersonal issue, schedule a private conversation with both students involved.
A student on a team of 5 has only 3 teammates rate them (one absent, one missed the form) Peer average is based on the 3 submitted ratings. Still valid. Document the incomplete data.
A student requests to see their peer ratings Decline. Share the aggregated percentage and the rubric, but not individual ratings or rater identities. This is the confidentiality promise you made in the syllabus.
