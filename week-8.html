Thursday TBL: investigate the differential

Five options. Anatomy decides.

Each option below could produce gait disturbance, cognitive change, or bladder changes in an older adult. Your team's job is to anatomically distinguish them using ventricular size, periventricular tract involvement, and the LP / tap-test response.

Option Anatomic site / mechanism Distinguishing feature
Normal pressure hydrocephalus (NPH) Ventricular dilation compresses periventricular tracts Wet / wobbly / wacky triad + ventriculomegaly disproportionate to atrophy + tap-test response
Alzheimer's disease Cortical atrophy, hippocampal predominance Memory predominates, gait late finding, atrophy proportional to ventricular size
Parkinson's disease Substantia nigra dopaminergic loss Tremor at rest, rigidity, bradykinesia, asymmetric onset, levodopa-responsive
Vascular (multi-infarct) dementia Multiple small infarcts in white matter / subcortical structures Stepwise decline, focal neurologic findings, MRI shows multifocal infarcts
Subdural hematoma Subdural space (between dura and arachnoid) Often history of fall or trauma, lateralizing signs, hyperdense or mixed-density collection on imaging

Team task

  1. For each option, locate the anatomic site on your master diagram.
  2. Mark which case findings are consistent and inconsistent with each option.
  3. Pick number one. Defend it using the anatomic discriminator.
  4. Be ready to explain why each rejected option does not fit, anatomically.
