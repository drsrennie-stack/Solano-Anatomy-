Thursday TBL: investigate the differential

Five options. Anatomy decides.

Each option below could produce blistering and skin loss. Your team's job is to anatomically distinguish them using the layer where separation occurs and the BSA criterion.

Option Anatomic site / mechanism Distinguishing feature
Stevens-Johnson Syndrome Dermal-epidermal junction Less than 10 percent BSA
Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis Dermal-epidermal junction Greater than 30 percent BSA
Staphylococcal Scalded Skin Syndrome Granular layer (intra-epidermal) Toxin-mediated, often pediatric
Pemphigus vulgaris Suprabasal (intra-epidermal) Autoimmune, oral lesions first
Bullous pemphigoid Sub-epidermal Tense bullae, elderly population

Team task

  1. For each option, locate the anatomic site on your master diagram.
  2. Mark which case findings are consistent and inconsistent with each option.
  3. Pick number one. Defend it using the anatomic discriminator.
  4. Be ready to explain why each rejected option does not fit, anatomically.
