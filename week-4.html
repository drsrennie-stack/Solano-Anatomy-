Thursday TBL: investigate the differential

Five options. Anatomy decides.

Each option below is a cyanotic congenital heart lesion. Your team's job is to anatomically distinguish them using the echo findings and the embryologic developmental defect.

Option Anatomic site / mechanism Distinguishing feature
Tetralogy of Fallot Anterior malalignment of conal septum VSD + overriding aorta + RVH + pulmonary stenosis (the four findings)
Tricuspid atresia Absent tricuspid valve Hypoplastic RV, requires ASD + VSD for survival, single ventricle physiology
Transposition of great vessels Failed conotruncal rotation Aorta from RV, pulmonary artery from LV, immediate cyanosis at birth
Truncus arteriosus Failed septation of truncus Single great vessel arises from heart, large VSD, mixed circulation
Total anomalous pulmonary venous return Pulmonary veins drain abnormally Pulmonary veins to systemic venous circulation, snowman silhouette

Team task

  1. For each option, locate the anatomic site on your master diagram.
  2. Mark which case findings are consistent and inconsistent with each option.
  3. Pick number one. Defend it using the anatomic discriminator.
  4. Be ready to explain why each rejected option does not fit, anatomically.
